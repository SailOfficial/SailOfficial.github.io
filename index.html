Virginia Plan

The Virginia Plan seeks to eliminate the disunity found within our young nation while still giving the individuals a voice, by instituting a powerful federal government, elected through proportial representation.

Representation

The Virginia Plan eliminates our initial complaint the British by allowing the individuals to vote on representatives in legislation and even allows for the proper representation of small states using a proportional representation system.  

Unification

By allowing the federal government more power over the states, we are encouraging our states to grow closer and work for the good of each other.  

Protection

The Virginia plan allows for the creation of a powerful armed forces providing the protection of our individual liberties and defending us from possible military conflicts.

Danger:  The New Jersey Plan

When presented with our mighty proposal at the Constitutional Convention, a few corrupt and power-hungry delegates from the smaller states were quick to object and hastily scurried away in an attempt to mash together a flimsy excuse for a government that the megalomaniacs refer to as the “New Jersey Plan”. This Frankenstein of a plan is fraught with dangerous propositions that attack the fundamentals principles of these united states and hand power to a select few.

Whereas the Virginia Plan advocates for proportional representation in legislature, the opposition insists on “equal” representation, a practice that leads, for instance, a citizen in Delaware to have more than twelve times the representation of a citizen of Virginia!

Fundamentally, the New Jersey plan focuses on securing the powers of the states, which are nothing more than arbitrary geographic divisions of an otherwise united people, put in place by a tyrant from a bygone era (King George III). Vote for the rights of the people, not the states!

This New Jersey Plan also seemingly glances over the pressing issue of national security, proposing the fledgling state militias as the primary means to secure our place in this new world. And when our nation is inevitably provoked in a war of commerce, what are we to do if we cannot impose tariffs and regulate the trade of our poor nation?

For all these reasons and more, the New Jersey Plan is a great threat to the integrity of this fledgling nation, seeking to temporarily hand power to an elect few before the United States are splintered away into oblivion.
